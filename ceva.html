Discord


Task #1: Observe the existing affordances, visual variables and interaction flows for a given use case – for example, delivering an academic presentation or attending a specific meeting at late hours – and propose better approaches (if any).


  • Existing affordances:


      Voice and video chat: Discord allows users to engage in high-quality voice and video chats with friends, colleagues, or members of their community.

      Text chat: Users can also send text messages to individuals or groups, making it easy to keep in touch with others.

      File sharing: Discord provides a platform for sharing files, images, videos, and other media with others.

      Screen sharing: Discord allows users to share their screen with others during a voice or video chat, which is helpful for collaboration and remote work.

      Server creation and management: Users can create their own servers and customize them to suit their needs. They can also manage their servers by setting roles, permissions, and channels.

      Bots: Discord supports a wide range of bots that can be used to automate tasks, manage servers, and enhance the user experience.


  • Visual variables:


      Color: Discord uses a variety of colors to differentiate different channels, users, roles, and statuses. For example, channels are colored based on their category and users have different colors based on their roles. Discord's logo also uses a distinct combination of blue, white, and purple.

      Shape: Discord's user interface includes various shapes, such as rectangular text boxes, circular avatars, and square buttons. Each shape serves a specific purpose, such as the rounded edges of the text boxes helping to visually separate messages in a chat.

      Size: Discord utilizes size to indicate the relative importance of different elements. For example, larger fonts are used for headings and channel names, while smaller fonts are used for individual messages. Additionally, the size of user avatars and emojis can indicate their level of importance in a chat.

      Contrast: Contrast is used throughout Discord to improve readability. For example, the contrast between light text on a dark background or dark text on a light background is used to make text easier to read.

      Motion: Discord uses motion to provide feedback and create a sense of interactivity. For example, when a user joins a voice chat, their avatar will move to the chat room to visually indicate their presence.

      Texture: Discord uses texture to create the illusion of dimensionality in its design. For example, the background of Discord's user interface includes a subtle pattern that adds visual interest and helps to visually separate different sections of the app. Additionally, emojis and icons have a distinct texture that helps them stand out from other elements.


  • Interaction flows:


      Joining and leaving servers: To join a new server, users typically either receive an invitation link from another user or search for public servers in Discord's server directory. Once they have joined a server, users can easily leave by clicking on the server name and selecting "Leave Server".

      Navigating channels: Discord's channels allow users to communicate with others in different chat rooms. Users can navigate between channels by clicking on their names in the channel list, or by typing in the channel name in the search bar.

      Sending messages: Users can send messages in Discord by typing into the text box at the bottom of a channel. They can also include emojis, GIFs, and attachments to their messages.

      Voice and video chat: Discord offers voice and video chat features for users to communicate with others in real-time. Users can join a voice or video chat by clicking on the relevant channel and clicking the "Join Voice" or "Join Video" button.

      Managing user settings: Users can customize their Discord experience by adjusting their settings, such as changing their username, profile picture, and notification preferences. These settings can be accessed by clicking on the user icon in the bottom left corner of the screen and selecting "User Settings".

      Moderating servers: Server administrators and moderators have additional tools and settings to manage their servers, such as creating roles and permissions, managing bans and warnings, and adjusting channel settings.


Task #2: Compare the visual metaphors and idioms and suggest the similar ones for a text-only interaction (see CLI – Command Line Interface).


  • Visual metaphors and idioms:


      Icons - used to indicate different actions or functions, (i.e. a microphone icon to indicate that a user is speaking in a voice chat). In a text-only interaction, these icons could be replaced with text labels, such as "Speaking" or "Muted".

      Emojis - used to express emotions and reactions to messages. In a text-only interaction, similar concepts could be represented using text-based emoticons, such as ":-)" for a smile or ":-(" for a sad face.

      Colors: Discord uses colors to differentiate between different channels and user roles. In a text-only interaction, similar concepts could be conveyed using text formatting, such as bold or italic text to indicate important information.

      Hierarchy: Discord uses size and placement to indicate the hierarchy of different elements, such as larger fonts for channel names and smaller fonts for individual messages. In a text-only interaction, similar concepts could be replaced using text formatting or text-colors to indicate different levels of importance.

      Motion: Discord uses animation and motion to manifest in a natural way what is happening at the moment, for example the Discord logo rolling while loading, or the caller's profile picture pulsating while calling. In a text-only interaction, similar concepts could be represented using text-based feedback, such as "Loading... / Calling..." to indicate the same functionalities.


Task #3: Discuss the cognitive overload in special conditions/contexts.


  • Cognitive overload:


      Multiple servers and channels: Discord allows users to join multiple servers and channels, which can sometimes result in information overload. Users may find it difficult to keep track of all the different conversations and topics happening at once, which can be overwhelming.

      Notifications: Discord has a notification system that alerts users to new messages, mentions, and other events. While this can be useful, it can also lead to cognitive overload if users receive too many notifications at once.

      Bots: Discord supports a wide range of bots that can enhance the platform's functionality. However, users may once again find it difficult to keep track of all the different bots and commands, which can lead to cognitive overload.

      Customization options: Discord offers a high degree of customization, allowing users to create their own servers, channels, and roles. While this can be useful, it can also lead to cognitive overload if users are presented with too many options and decisions to make. For example, the User Settings tab comes from the start with 20+ categories of settings to be chosen from, which will trigger certain settings once clicked, and so on.

Task #6: List three new features that can be added to improve the experience and sketch those new features on paper and/or digitally.


  • Three new features:


      1. One of the main new features we would add is a "chat filtering" option. This would prevent spam messages from flooding a server, moderate content in order to enforce community guidelines, protect users against malicious links and phishing attempts.

      2. Another feature is an "edit history", since Discord allows users to edit messages, but not to see the previous edited message. Zoom


Task #1: Observe the existing affordances, visual variables and interaction flows for a given use case – for example, delivering an academic presentation or attending a specific meeting at late hours – and propose better approaches (if any).


  • Existing affordances:


      Video and Audio: Zoom provides high-quality video and audio conferencing features that enable users to communicate with each other in real-time. Users can join meetings from their desktop, laptop, tablet, or smartphone.

      Screen Sharing: Zoom allows users to share their screen with others, making it easy to collaborate on presentations, documents, or other visual aids.

      Recording: Zoom provides the ability to record meetings, which can be useful for those who can't attend the meeting live or for those who want to review the content later.

      Chatting: Zoom has a chat feature that allows users to communicate via text messages during a meeting. This can be useful for sharing links, making notes, or asking questions.

      Virtual Backgrounds: Zoom offers the option to use virtual backgrounds, which can be useful for those who want to maintain privacy or to create a more professional appearance.

      Breakout Rooms: Zoom allows the host to create breakout rooms, which can be used for group discussions or activities during a meeting.

      Polls: Zoom offers the ability to create and conduct polls during a meeting, which can be used to gather feedback or to gauge the opinions of participants.


  • Visual variables:


      Color: The color scheme used in the interface can affect the user's mood and perception. Zoom uses a blue and white color scheme that is calming and professional.

      Size: The size of elements on the screen can influence how important they appear. For example, larger buttons and text may seem more important and draw the user's attention.

      Shape: The shape of elements can convey meaning and affect user perception. Zoom's rectangular buttons and windows create a structured and organized interface.

      Contrast: Contrast between elements can help users distinguish between different components of the interface. Zoom uses high contrast between buttons and background to make them stand out.

      Animation: Animations can be used to draw attention and create a sense of interactivity. Zoom uses animations to indicate when someone is speaking or to highlight the active speaker.

      Typography: The font type, size, and weight can affect readability. Zoom uses a simple font that is easy to read on different devices.


  • Interaction flows:


      Joining a meeting: Zoom makes it easy for users to join meetings by providing a simple and clear interface that allows users to enter a meeting ID or use a link to join.

      Audio and video controls: it allows users to adjust their microphone and camera settings. This is important as it impacts the user's ability to communicate effectively during a meeting.

      Screen sharing: is a key feature of Zoom that allows users to share their screen with others. This is crucial for collaborative work and presentations.

      Chatting: Zoom's chat feature allows users to communicate with other participants during a meeting. This is useful for sharing links, files, or other information with other attendees.

      Recording: Zoom's recording feature allows users to record meetings for later reference. This can impact user perception by providing a sense of security and accountability.

      Leaving a meeting: The final interaction flow is leaving a meeting. Zoom makes it easy for users to leave a meeting by providing a clear and simple interface.


Task #2: Compare the visual metaphors and idioms and suggest the similar ones for a text-only interaction (see CLI – Command Line Interface).


  • Visual metaphors and idioms:


      Gallery view: it displays participants in a grid-like layout, a physical gallery of people. This layout allows users to see multiple participants at once, making it easier to follow group conversations.

      Speaker view: Zoom's speaker view highlights the active speaker, focusing on the person speaking in a physical meeting. This layout helps users to stay engaged and follow the conversation more easily.

      Raise hand: Zoom's "raise hand" feature is an idiom for signaling a desire to speak in a group conversation. This feature allows users to virtually raise their hand, indicating that they have something to contribute.

      Chat bubble: the chat feature uses a chat bubble icon, which is a visual metaphor for speech bubbles in comics or cartoons. This icon helps users to recognize the chat feature and understand its purpose.

      Stop video: Zoom's "stop video" feature uses a camera icon with a diagonal line across it, which is an idiom for a camera being turned off. This icon helps users to recognize that their video feed is turned off.

      End meeting: Zoom's "end meeting" feature uses an icon of a door, which is an idiom for leaving a physical meeting. This icon helps users to recognize the end of a meeting and to exit the call.


Task #3: Discuss the cognitive overload in special conditions/contexts.


  • Cognitive overload:


      Multiple participants: Zoom can display up to 49 participants in gallery view, which can be overwhelming and make it difficult for users to focus on the conversation.

      Multiple modes of communication: Zoom offers multiple modes of communication, including audio, video, chat, and screen sharing. Managing these different modes of communication can be challenging and lead to cognitive overload.
